BIO

Sasha Behar is a Franco American-writer and occasional painter, raised in Paris’s least cinematic arrondissement (the 15th). Her worldview was shaped by three steady forces: Catalan hedonism, Irish and Jewish guilt, and the unpredictable logic of an alcoholic parent.

Her love of film began at age five when she accompanied her carpenter father to work on the set of Marie Antoinette. She fell in love like you fall off a roof: suddenly, and with no real plan for the landing. A graduate of the Sorbonne with High Honors in English and History, Behar has worked across film and television in both narrative and branded productions for Disney+, HBO, Gaumont, Prime Video, Chanel, and Louis Vuitton.

She has written two short films, two original pilots, and three feature-length screenplays, with recurring themes of dysfunctional families, class rage, cultural displacement, and the quiet (or not so quiet) violence of trying to belong. She has a soft spot for children of chaos, failed revolutionaries, and people who love each other badly. She is currently completing her first novel, Canaries.
